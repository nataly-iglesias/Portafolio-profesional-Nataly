Online Flower Shop: Raíz y Pétalo

About the project

Conceptual project for a fictional flower shop focused on transforming online flower shopping into a visually appealing and emotionally significant experience, where the product and the intention of the gift are the protagonists.

Problem

Many digital flower shops have interfaces that are unattractive, difficult to navigate, or have confusing purchasing processes. This makes it difficult for users to quickly find what they need and reduces the emotional value of the purchase.

Target user

  • People looking to give flowers as a gift.
  • Users who want to buy quickly and without complications.
  • Customers who value visual, intuitive, and emotional experiences.

Design decisions

  • Use of large images to highlight the product and make it the protagonist.
  • Clear classifications to reduce cognitive load.
  • Purchase process guided by simple steps.
  • Soft color palette and elegant typography.

Design system

To maintain visual consistency and future development, a system of reusable components was established that allows structuring the interface in a clear and coherent way.

  • Primary and secondary buttons.
  • Product cards.
  • Form inputs.
  • State changes.
  • Navigation bar.

Solution

Raíz y Pétalo offers a minimalist interface focused on visuals and the simplicity of the purchase flow, which reduces navigation difficulty and allows the user to choose and give flowers quickly, safely, and pleasantly.

Financial App: MoneyWise

About the project

Interface design for a personal finance mobile application focused on simplifying money management and promoting a savings culture. Through the 50/30/20 method, the app allows users to plan their expenses, learn financial concepts, and track progress visually.

Problem

Most financial applications are perceived as complex and unfriendly for inexperienced users, especially young people. This creates a barrier that prevents many people from consistently tracking finances and limits the development of effective savings habits.

Target user

  • Beginners who want to start saving but don't know where to begin.
  • Young people looking for a simple, friendly, and accessible financial solution.

Design decisions

  • Gamification of progress through tracking bars and reminders.
  • Section of short, interactive courses to promote financial education.
  • Pastel color palette to reduce the feeling of using a complex financial application.
  • Line art style for a clean and tidy appearance.

Solution

MoneyWise is proposed as a mobile application that reduces the complexity of financial management through a structure based on the 50/30/20 method, combined with visual elements and dynamics that facilitate understanding and encourage consistency. The experience prioritizes user support, combining learning and habit tracking.

Food App: CareBites

About the project

Design of a mobile application aimed at connecting local donors and beneficiaries, allowing the publication of surplus food in good condition to facilitate its donation or low-cost sale through an accessible and efficient system.

Problem

Food waste represents a serious problem in Mexico, where millions of tons are discarded each year. Despite the existence of some similar initiatives, they do not address the local context or effectively serve small businesses and individuals/organizations that could directly benefit from these resources.

Target user

  • Individual beneficiaries.
  • Collective beneficiaries (social organizations).
  • Donors (companies, restaurants, and businesses).

Design decisions

  • Use of a warm color palette and sans-serif typography.
  • Application of usability heuristic principles and accessibility criteria.
  • Design of a simple interface that facilitates the management of key actions such as publishing, searching, and requesting products.

User flow

A user flow was designed that prioritizes simplicity and efficiency in key actions, allowing users to publish, search, and request products quickly and without complications.

Design system

A system of reusable components was developed to ensure visual consistency and scalability in the interface.

  • Fluid navigation bar.
  • Product cards with key information.
  • Primary and secondary buttons.
  • Inputs and forms.
  • State changes and animations.

Solution

A mobile application is proposed that facilitates direct connection between donors and beneficiaries through a simple system for publishing and searching for food, integrating personalized notifications according to user needs, clear product information, and an order history that allows tracking interactions.
